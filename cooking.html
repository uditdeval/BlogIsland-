Here's a blog post on "How to Make a Classic Spaghetti Bolognese." --- ## How to Make a Classic Spaghetti Bolognese Welcome back to [Your Cooking Blog Name]! Today, we're taking a trip to Italy in the comfort of your own kitchen to whip up a timeless favorite - Spaghetti Bolognese. This hearty and flavorful pasta dish is perfect for family dinners, date nights, or whenever you're craving a comforting meal. Let's dive right in! ### Ingredients: - 1 lb (450g) ground beef - 1 medium onion, finely chopped - 2 cloves garlic, minced - 1 carrot, finely chopped - 1 celery stalk, finely chopped - 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes - 2 tablespoons tomato paste - 1/2 cup red wine (optional) - 1/2 cup whole milk - 1 teaspoon dried oregano - 1 teaspoon dried basil - Salt and pepper to taste - Olive oil - 1 lb (450g) spaghetti - Grated Parmesan cheese (for serving) - Fresh basil leaves (for garnish) ### Instructions: #### 1. Sauté the Aromatics: Start by heating a tablespoon of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, garlic, carrot, and celery. Sauté until the vegetables are softened and the onions become translucent, about 5 minutes. #### 2. Brown the Ground Beef: Push the sautéed vegetables to the side of the skillet and add the ground beef to the other side. Break up the meat with a wooden spoon and cook until it's browned and no longer pink. #### 3. Combine and Season: Mix the browned beef with the sautéed vegetables. Stir in the tomato paste, dried oregano, and dried basil. Season generously with salt and pepper. #### 4. Add the Liquid: Pour in the red wine (if using) and let it simmer for a few minutes to reduce slightly. Then, add the crushed tomatoes and whole milk. Stir well to combine all the ingredients. #### 5. Simmer: Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet, and let the sauce simmer for at least 30 minutes, but longer if you have time (1-2 hours is ideal). This slow simmering allows the flavors to meld and intensify. #### 6. Cook the Spaghetti: While the sauce is simmering, cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside. #### 7. Serve: Once the sauce has reached your desired consistency, taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. Spoon a generous amount of the sauce over a serving of cooked spaghetti. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and grated Parmesan cheese. #### 8. Enjoy: Serve your classic Spaghetti Bolognese hot and enjoy this comforting Italian masterpiece! ### Tips: - If you prefer a vegetarian version, you can substitute the ground beef with textured vegetable protein (TVP) or finely chopped mushrooms for a rich, meaty texture. - Don't forget to sprinkle extra Parmesan cheese on top before digging in—it adds a delightful salty kick. - Leftovers can be refrigerated and taste even better the next day, so make extra for lunch!
